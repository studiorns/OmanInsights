Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

91.37% (+3.07pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 91.37%, Oman has excellent awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating market saturation at the awareness level.
  • The +3.07pp year-over-year growth suggests continued improvement despite already high awareness levels.
  • With awareness exceeding 90%, marketing efforts should focus less on building awareness and more on driving consideration and intent.
  • The high awareness level provides a strong foundation for conversion-focused marketing strategies.
  • Given the proximity of Oman to Abu Dhabi and cultural similarities, this high awareness is expected but still impressive.
  • Marketing should leverage this high awareness by focusing on specific experiences and offerings rather than general destination awareness.

Consideration

53.10% (+4.10pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 53.10%, consideration is in the good range, indicating a competitive position in the Omani traveler's mindset.
  • The +4.10pp increase shows strong positive momentum in consideration growth.
  • There is still a significant gap between awareness (91.37%) and consideration (53.10%), indicating that while most Omani travelers know about Abu Dhabi, only about half are actively considering it as a destination.
  • The positive trend in consideration (+4.10pp) suggests that marketing efforts to improve consideration are working effectively.
  • Marketing should continue to focus on addressing the specific barriers that prevent aware consumers from considering Abu Dhabi, particularly expense concerns (48%) and preference for other destinations (38%).
  • The consideration level above 50% provides a solid foundation for conversion-focused marketing strategies.

Intent

36.89% (+1.89pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 36.89%, intent is in the good range, indicating healthy conversion potential from consideration to actual travel planning.
  • The +1.89pp increase shows positive momentum, though at a slower rate than consideration growth (+4.10pp).
  • The gap between consideration (53.10%) and intent (36.89%) suggests that there are still barriers to converting consideration into actual travel plans.
  • The intent level above 35% is strong for a neighboring market where repeat visitation may be a factor (33% have already visited).
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking, particularly expense concerns (48%).
  • The positive trend in intent (+1.89pp) suggests that tactical, conversion-focused campaigns are working and should be continued.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.69 (-0.02)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 36.89% ÷ 53.10% = 0.69

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.69, the conversion from consideration to intent is good, indicating that 69% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The slight decrease of -0.02 suggests a minor decline in conversion efficiency, likely due to consideration growing faster (+4.10pp) than intent (+1.89pp).
  • This good ratio suggests that once Omani travelers consider Abu Dhabi, they have a relatively high likelihood of converting to actual intent to visit.
  • The ratio is among the highest in the region, indicating strong conversion efficiency compared to other markets.
  • Marketing should focus on maintaining this strong conversion rate while continuing to grow the consideration pool.
  • The slight decline in this ratio (-0.02) should be monitored to ensure it doesn't represent the beginning of a negative trend.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Oman market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

1.6 days

The average number of nights Omani travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 1.6-day average stay indicates a very short trip duration, suggesting that Oman is primarily a short-break or weekend destination market.
  • This short stay duration is likely influenced by the proximity of Oman to Abu Dhabi, making it easy for quick visits.
  • Marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 1-2 day timeframe.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend packages specifically designed for the Omani market.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 1-2 day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The very short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time.
  • There's also an opportunity to develop strategies to extend the average stay duration, potentially by bundling attractions or creating multi-day event packages.

Booking Window

35 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Omani travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 35-day booking window is moderate, indicating a market that plans trips with reasonable lead time but not far in advance.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 1-2 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for some consideration and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Last-minute promotions and offers may be effective for this market, given the relatively short booking window.
  • The moderate booking window suggests that digital media should be prioritized over traditional media, as it allows for more immediate response to marketing messages.
  • This planning period provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements, but decisions need to be made relatively quickly.
Strategic Example:

For the July-August peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in May to align with the 35-day booking window. Flash sales and limited-time offers could be particularly effective for this market, creating urgency within the typical decision-making timeframe.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Jul & Aug

The months with the highest volume of Omani hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Omani guests.

Business Implications:
  • July (11,124 guests) and August (13,305 guests) represent the clear peak season for Omani travelers.
  • This summer peak aligns with school holidays in Oman and the desire to escape to air-conditioned indoor attractions during the hottest months.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (June, September) to extend the peak periods.
  • The concentration of visits in summer months suggests that indoor attractions and activities should be highlighted in marketing materials.
  • The significant drop in visitation during March-April represents an opportunity for growth through targeted campaigns highlighting spring activities and events.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.7 (moderate seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Oman travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if July has a travel query index of 27.0 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 15.9, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.7 (27.0 ÷ 15.9).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.7 value indicates that the market has high seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 70% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 35-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The high seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.
  • The concentration in summer months (July-August) indicates that indoor attractions and activities should be emphasized in marketing materials for the peak season.

Media Elasticity

0.38 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Oman market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.38 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 3.8%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive and solidly in the moderate range (above 0.3).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI but with moderate efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries, but will produce modest growth.
  • The moderate elasticity suggests that the Oman market is somewhat responsive to media campaigns, but other factors also significantly influence travel decisions.
  • This moderate responsiveness suggests that media investments should be complemented by other marketing approaches, such as addressing specific barriers to visitation, particularly expense concerns (48%).
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign targeting the Oman market, you can expect approximately a 7.6% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.38 = 7.6%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.72 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Oman travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.72 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with good confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with greater certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.72) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±8.2%.
  • The high consistency suggests that the 44.8% growth forecast for 2025 can be considered reliable, though still subject to the confidence interval.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±8.2%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±8.2% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 137.7 and 162.3 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Oman market.
  • The 44.8% growth forecast for 2025 has a 95% confidence range of approximately 36.6% to 53.0%.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.
  • The interval width (8.2%) aligns with the high YoY growth consistency (0.72), providing further validation of the forecast reliability.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Omani travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Expense Concerns: 48% (+6pp) believe it is too expensive
  • Preference for Other Destinations: 38% (+5pp) would rather visit other places
  • Age Perception: 35% (+2pp) believe it is only for older people
  • Lack of Knowledge: 35% (+7pp) do not know enough about this place
  • Previous Visitation: 33% (-1pp) have already visited Abu Dhabi before
Business Implications:
  • Expense Concerns (48%, +6pp): This significant increase indicates growing price sensitivity in the Oman market. Marketing should emphasize value for money and consider developing more affordable package options specifically for this market.
  • Preference for Other Destinations (38%, +5pp): This increase suggests growing competition from alternative destinations. Marketing should emphasize Abu Dhabi's unique selling points and differentiation from competing destinations.
  • Age Perception (35%, +2pp): The perception that Abu Dhabi is "only for older people" is a significant barrier, particularly for a market that shows strong interest in theme parks and entertainment. Marketing should highlight the family-friendly and youth-oriented attractions.
  • Lack of Knowledge (35%, +7pp): This substantial increase suggests a need for more educational marketing content about Abu Dhabi's offerings. Awareness is high (91.37%), but specific knowledge about attractions and experiences may be lacking.
  • Previous Visitation (33%, -1pp): The slight decrease suggests that repeat visitation is becoming less of a barrier. Marketing should continue to highlight new attractions and experiences to encourage return visits.
  • The combination of expense concerns and preference for other destinations suggests a value perception issue that needs to be addressed through targeted marketing.
Strategic Recommendation:

Develop a "Value Discovery" campaign specifically for the Oman market that highlights affordable package options bundling accommodation with theme park tickets. Focus on showcasing the entertainment options that appeal to younger travelers to counter the age perception barrier. Include educational content about the variety of experiences available to address the knowledge gap. Emphasize new attractions and experiences to encourage those who have previously visited to return.

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Oman. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Oman market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Excellent awareness (91.37%) with good consideration (53.10%) and intent (36.89%), indicating a strong foundation but still significant barriers to conversion
  • A good intent-to-consideration ratio (0.69) indicating efficient funnel conversion once consideration is achieved
  • A very short average stay (1.6 days) indicating weekend or short-break vacation patterns
  • A moderate booking window (35 days) allowing for effective campaign planning
  • Strong summer seasonality with peaks in July and August
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.38) suggesting reasonable response to media investments
  • Strong interest in theme parks and entertainment attractions, with Ferrari World, Green Mubazzarah, and SeaWorld being the most searched attractions
  • Preference for Yas Island accommodations, with W Abu Dhabi Yas Island being particularly popular
  • Significant barriers including expense concerns (48%), preference for other destinations (38%), and age perception (35%)
  • Concerning growth in lack of knowledge (+7pp), laws and customs concerns (+7pp), and expense concerns (+6pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Developing value-focused weekend packages that bundle accommodation with theme park tickets to address expense concerns and leverage the short 1.6-day average stay
  • Creating educational content that highlights the variety of experiences available in Abu Dhabi to address the growing knowledge gap
  • Emphasizing the family-friendly and youth-oriented attractions to counter the perception that Abu Dhabi is "only for older people"
  • Highlighting the recent liberalization of laws and customs to address growing concerns in this area
  • Focusing marketing efforts on Yas Island attractions and accommodations, which align with Omani travelers' preferences
  • Timing campaigns to run 1-2 months before the July-August peak period, considering the 35-day booking window
  • Creating marketing content that showcases the entertainment options and shopping experiences that appeal to the Oman market
  • Developing partnerships with popular accommodations like W Abu Dhabi Yas Island to create targeted offers
  • Leveraging the proximity of Al Ain attractions to the Oman border for day-trip promotions
